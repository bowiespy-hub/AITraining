THE REALITY CHECK

Magic Ball vs. Intern

  • Stop treating AI like a crystal ball. It cannot read your mind.
  • Treat it like a brilliant intern. Infinite knowledge, but zero context.
  • Provide constraints. It needs clear directions to succeed.

The Output Gap

Why most agents sound like robots.

The Average User

  • Provides zero context.
  • Asks vague questions.
"Write an attractive post for a new project near KLCC."

The Professional

  • Defines the exact audience.
  • Demands a specific output format.
"Act as a consultant. Write a LinkedIn post targeting foreign investors focused on rental yield..."

Stop Repeating Yourself

Use Custom Instructions (ChatGPT) or Project Instructions (Claude) to permanently load your "Tone & Manner" into the AI.

How to Setup Your Tone & Manner

Paste these 3 dimensions into your Custom Instructions settings.

1. The Persona

  • Define your exact role.
  • "I am a PropNex consultant specializing in HNWI and foreign investments in Kuala Lumpur."

2. Guardrails

  • List your banned words.
  • "BANNED WORDS: Luxurious, Unbelievable, Don't miss out. Do not use generic marketing fluff."

3. Output Rules

  • Set default priorities.
  • "Always prioritize hard data. Highlight ROI, PSF comparisons, and Rental Yield upfront."

Deconstructing the Formula

What to focus on for each element.

Context

The Raw Material.

Provide location, facts, constraints, or the client's current situation.

Task

The Action.

Be specific: "Summarize", "Draft an email", "Create a table".

WHO

The Pain Point.

Identify the target reader and what they care about most (e.g., Yield vs. Lifestyle).

Tone

The Anchor.

Set the vibe: Empathetic, highly analytical, or urgent.

Format

The Boundaries.

Set hard limits: Max 150 words, 3 bullet points, or CSV output.
